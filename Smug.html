Business Problem

Some SMU SCIS students may require additional academic help with specific mods but are too embarrassed to seek help from their teaching assistants or professors. Other existing avenues of help such as mentoring groups are not effective in bridging the academic gap either. Consequently, students who need additional academic support are not receiving it , and hence will be lagging behind academically.

Why SMUG?

SMU students who have taken and excelled in modules are incentivized to offer their tutoring services on SMUG at a price. Struggling students who need additional academic help would be more comfortable seeking help from tutors as they are not in common circles of friends. This is due to the awkwardness of repeatedly having to approach them for help, as well as the sensitivity when it comes to incentivizing a tutoring session. Tutors and tutees would communicate on SMUG and have a consensus on 1) topics to be tutored, 2) date and time of tuition, 3) fee to be paid upfront through SMUG.
