Zen Buddy

Mobile App Interactive Prototyping

Gen Zs are aware of the benefits of meditation but are either too busy or not incentivised to do so. The lack of tangible instant benefits coupled with the lack of knowledge on how to meditate deters them from meditating on a regular basis.
Our teammates came up with "ZenBuddy" which is a mobile app that allows user to complete bite-sized meditations and compete with friends. Zen Buddy provides audio cues, visuals and a progress report that guides user to meditate effectively.

Design System

By creating reusable design system, we could focus less on tweaking visual appearance and more on complex problems (like information prioritization, workflow optimization, and journey management). While this payoff might seem small when creating only a small number of screens, I learnt that it becomes substantial when we need to coordinate efforts within the team and various screens.

Parallel prototyping

We chose parallel design for selected screens as parallel design allows Concepts generated to be combined so that the final solution benefits from all ideas proposed. After me and my groupmates came up with different designs and functionalities for each screen we came up with finalised screens as you can see.

Prototyping

Using Figma, we created screens with various functionalities to target our persona who doesn't know how to meditate, needs incentive to meditate, and unable to find time to meditate.

Experiment

Lastly, we conducted experiment using Maze to test the overall ease of task completion, satisfaction with completion time, and satisfaction with support information

Reflection

From this project, I learnt that it is important to relate, understand and address the persona's pain points. Moreover, exercising empathy is the foundation in design process and testing rigorously is important as opinions and judgements on designs can vary greatly from experimental results
